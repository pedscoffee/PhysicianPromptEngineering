TWEET 2/10:
The 18 HIPAA identifiers to NEVER include in AI prompts: Names, dates (except year), phone numbers, email, SSN, MRN, health plan numbers, account numbers...

---

TWEET 3/10:
...device identifiers, URLs, IP addresses, biometric IDs, photos, geographic subdivisions smaller than state, and any unique identifying characteristic.

---

TWEET 4/10:
Safe Harbor method: Remove all 18 identifiers. What remains is de-identified and can be used more freely (but still use judgment).

---

TWEET 5/10:
Business Associate Agreements (BAA): If AI vendor handles PHI, they need a BAA with your organization. Consumer AI tools = no BAA = don't use with PHI.

---

TWEET 6/10:
Minimum necessary standard: Only use the minimum information needed to generate your documentation. Don't paste entire charts.

---

TWEET 7/10:
Access controls: Use secure devices, strong passwords, automatic logout. Physical security matters for AI documentation tools.

---

TWEET 8/10:
Audit trails: Some organizations require logging AI tool usage. Check your institution's requirements.

---

TWEET 9/10:
State laws: Some states have stricter privacy laws than HIPAA. California, Texas, New York have additional requirements.

---

TWEET 10/10:
When in doubt, consult your compliance officer. Better to ask than to cause a breach. Your medical license is worth the conversation.
