TWEET 1/8:
Your first AI prompt will feel awkward. You're essentially teaching a computer how you think clinically. Here's the anatomy of an effective medical documentation prompt. ðŸ§µ

---

TWEET 2/8:
Element 1: Role definition. Start with "You are an experienced [specialty] physician working in [setting]." This sets the clinical context.

---

TWEET 3/8:
Element 2: Task description. "Your task is to generate a [note type] for [patient scenario]." Be specific about what you want.

---

TWEET 4/8:
Element 4: Format requirements. "Use SOAP format. HPI should be 3-5 sentences. Include pertinent positives and negatives."

---

TWEET 5/8:
Element 5: Examples (few-shot learning). Paste 2-3 of your actual notes as examples. "Here are examples of my documentation style: [examples]"

---

TWEET 6/8:
Element 6: Constraints. "Do not speculate beyond given information. Do not include patient identifiers. Maximum 300 words."

---

TWEET 7/8:
Element 7: Input format. End with "Given the following clinical information: [paste de-identified details here]"

---

TWEET 8/8:
Put it together and test. Expect iteration. Your 10th version will be much better than your first. That's normal and expected.
