TWEET 2/9:
Start small: One note type, master it, then expand. Trying to automate everything at once leads to burnout and abandonment.

---

TWEET 3/9:
Schedule prompt reviews: Quarterly reviews of your prompt library. What's working? What's outdated? What needs refinement?

---

TWEET 4/9:
Stay current: AI capabilities evolve rapidly. What failed 6 months ago might work now. Revisit abandoned approaches.

---

TWEET 5/9:
Community connection: Join physician AI groups. Share experiences, learn from others, stay motivated.

---

TWEET 6/9:
Measure continuously: Track time savings, satisfaction, quality metrics. Data keeps you accountable and motivated.

---

TWEET 7/9:
Teach others: Explaining your process to colleagues reinforces your understanding and improves your practice.

---

TWEET 8/9:
Accept imperfection: Some notes will always be faster manually. AI augments, doesn't replace all documentation.

---

TWEET 9/9:
Remember why: Sustainable practice, work-life balance, joy in medicine. AI is a tool toward these goals, not the goal itself.
