Problem: We think through complex differentials but document only the final conclusion. Chart doesn't reflect our cognitive work.

---

TWEET 3/8:
Medical-legal risk: "Why didn't you consider X?" If it's not documented, it's hard to prove you considered it.

---

TWEET 4/8:
AI prompt approach: "Document my clinical reasoning process including differential diagnosis, key decision points, and why alternatives were excluded."

---

TWEET 5/8:
Example output: "Considered PE given chest pain and tachycardia, but low Wells score, normal O2 sat, and pleuritic nature favor MSK etiology."

---

TWEET 6/8:
This documents you DID consider serious diagnoses and had valid clinical reasoning for excluding them. Defensive medicine that's also good medicine.

---

TWEET 7/8:
For trainees: Visible clinical reasoning is teaching. Your notes become learning tools when they show diagnostic thought process.

---

TWEET 8/8:
AI helps structure and articulate reasoning you're already doing mentally. Makes your cognitive work visible and documentable.
