Start early: Interns should learn proper AI use alongside proper EMR use. It's a core clinical skill now, not a shortcut.

---

TWEET 3/7:
Teach principles first: De-identification, prompt engineering, output verification. Understanding beats rote copying.

---

TWEET 4/7:
Supervised practice: Residents draft prompts, attendings review both prompts and outputs. Double teaching opportunity.

---

TWEET 5/7:
Compare: Have residents write note manually, then with AI assistance. Discuss differences, what AI missed, what improved.

---

TWEET 6/7:
Competency: Residents should graduate able to write effective prompts, identify hallucinations, and use AI safely and efficiently.

---

TWEET 7/7:
Model good practice: If you use AI, be transparent about it. Show residents your prompts. Normalize AI as a tool, not a crutch.
