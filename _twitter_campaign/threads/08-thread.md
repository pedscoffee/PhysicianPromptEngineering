---

TWEET 2/8:
Start with your top 10 note types by volume. For family medicine: annual physical, diabetes follow-up, hypertension, sick visit, well child.

---

TWEET 3/8:
Create one file per prompt type. Name clearly: "FamilyMed_AnnualPhysical_Ages45-65.md" - you'll thank yourself later.

---

TWEET 4/8:
Include metadata in each prompt: specialty, visit type, typical patient age, required elements, last updated date.

---

TWEET 5/8:
Version your prompts. "AnnualPhysical_v3.md" lets you track improvements and roll back if needed.

---

TWEET 6/8:
Add a README to your library explaining your organization system and naming conventions. Future you will appreciate it.

---

TWEET 7/8:
Share prompts with colleagues in your specialty. Their feedback improves your prompts; your prompts improve their documentation.

---

TWEET 8/8:
Review and update quarterly. Clinical guidelines change, your practice evolves. Living library, not static archive.
