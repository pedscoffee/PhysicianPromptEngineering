# Physician Prompt Engineering Podcast Series

## Series Overview

**Title:** Physician Prompt Engineering: Reclaiming Time Through Better Documentation

**Format:** 8-episode limited series
**Length:** 20-30 minutes per episode
**Style:** Solo host, conversational, educational, practical
**Target Audience:** Physicians struggling with documentation burden

## Series Goals

1. **Educate** physicians about AI documentation tools
2. **Demystify** large language models and prompt engineering
3. **Provide** actionable steps to implement immediately
4. **Address** privacy, compliance, and safety concerns
5. **Build** community around physician-led innovation
6. **Inspire** hope that documentation burden is solvable

## Episode Arc

### Foundation (Episodes 1-2)
- Establish the problem and why it matters
- Demystify AI/LLMs as practical tools
- Set realistic expectations

### Implementation (Episodes 3-5)
- Practical how-to content
- Safety and compliance
- Customization for specialties

### Sustainability (Episodes 6-8)
- Advanced techniques
- Long-term workflow integration
- Community and future vision

## Episode List

1. **The Documentation Crisis** - Why this matters, personal story, the hidden cost
2. **Dictionary Math, Not Magic** - What LLMs really are, spell check analogy
3. **Your First Prompt** - Getting started practically, quick wins
4. **The Power of Examples** - Few-shot learning, why examples beat instructions
5. **Privacy, HIPAA, and Safety** - Doing this right, compliance, institutional approval
6. **Specialty Customization** - Making it work for your practice, advanced techniques
7. **Building a Sustainable Workflow** - Integration, iteration, team adoption
8. **Community-Driven Innovation** - Open source, sharing, the future

## Podcast Structure Template

Each episode follows this loose structure:

**Opening (2-3 min)**
- Hook: compelling question or story
- Episode topic introduction
- What you'll learn today

**Main Content (15-20 min)**
- 3-5 major talking points
- Personal stories and examples
- Practical demonstrations or walkthroughs
- Common pitfalls and how to avoid them

**Actionable Takeaway (3-5 min)**
- Specific next steps listeners can take
- Resources mentioned in episode
- Preview of next episode

**Closing (1-2 min)**
- Quick recap
- Call to action (website, community, contribution)
- Thank listeners

## Tone and Style

### Do's
✅ Be conversational and personal
✅ Share your own mistakes and learning
✅ Use real examples from clinical practice
✅ Acknowledge listener concerns and fears
✅ Keep it practical and immediately actionable
✅ Be honest about limitations
✅ Sound like you're talking to a colleague

### Don'ts
❌ Read from a script (use outlines only)
❌ Use too much technical jargon
❌ Oversell or hype AI capabilities
❌ Ignore legitimate concerns about AI
❌ Make it sound too easy
❌ Forget to address HIPAA/privacy
❌ Sound like a sales pitch

## Recording Tips

### Before Recording
- Review outline (don't memorize)
- Have 2-3 personal stories ready
- Test audio quality
- Minimize background noise
- Have water nearby

### During Recording
- Speak conversationally (imagine talking to one physician)
- Don't worry about "um" or "uh" (natural is good)
- Pause naturally between points
- Re-record sections that feel off
- Take breaks between sections if needed

### After Recording
- Light editing only (keep authentic feel)
- Add intro/outro music (optional)
- Normalize audio levels
- Add chapter markers for key sections
- Create show notes from outline

## Production Notes

### Equipment Needed (Minimum)
- Decent USB microphone or good headset
- Quiet recording space
- Free recording software (Audacity, GarageBand)
- Outline printed or on second screen

### Episode Preparation (30-45 min each)
1. Review episode outline
2. Identify 2-3 personal stories to share
3. Note any recent examples or news to mention
4. Have website open for referencing features
5. Test audio setup

### Recording Session (60-90 min each)
1. Record intro
2. Record main sections (can pause between)
3. Record actionable takeaway
4. Record outro
5. Re-record any sections that didn't flow

### Post-Production (30-60 min each)
1. Edit out long pauses, major mistakes
2. Normalize audio
3. Add intro/outro music if desired
4. Export to MP3
5. Create show notes from outline
6. Upload to podcast host
