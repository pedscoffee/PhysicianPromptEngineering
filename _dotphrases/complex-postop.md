## Wound Care

**Keep the surgical site clean and dry:**
1. Do not remove the dressing for **48 hours**
2. After 48 hours, you may shower (no baths/swimming for 2 weeks)
3. Pat dry gently - do not rub
4. Apply *prescribed antibiotic ointment* if ordered

**Watch for signs of infection:**
- Increasing redness or warmth around incision
- Foul-smelling or **yellow/green discharge**
- Fever >100.4°F (38°C)
- *Red streaks* extending from the wound

---

## Activity Restrictions

### Week 1-2
- **No lifting** >5 lbs
- Light walking encouraged
- *Avoid strenuous activity*

### Week 3-4
- Gradually increase activity
- May lift up to **10-15 lbs**
- Continue to avoid heavy exercise

### Week 5+
- Resume normal activities as tolerated
- Return to exercise with physician approval

---

## Medications

**Pain Management:**
- Take [MEDICATION] every [FREQUENCY] for pain
- Do not exceed maximum daily dose
- *Take with food* to prevent stomach upset

**Important:** Do not drive or operate machinery while taking narcotic pain medication.

---

## Diet

**First 24 hours:**
- Clear liquids only
- Advance as tolerated

**Days 2-7:**
- Regular diet as tolerated
- Avoid alcohol while on pain medication
- Stay well hydrated (8-10 glasses water/day)

## When to Call Us

**Call during office hours for:**
- Questions about medications
- Minor wound concerns
- Activity questions

**Call immediately or go to ER for:**
- **Severe pain** not controlled by medication
- Signs of infection (listed above)
- Difficulty breathing or chest pain
- Excessive bleeding from surgical site
- Nausea/vomiting preventing medication intake
