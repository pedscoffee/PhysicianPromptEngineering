## Your Learning Journey

This course teaches newborn physical exam skills through visual learning and clinical case analysis. You'll view images and scenarios of exam findings, practice identifying them, and receive immediate AI-powered feedback on your clinical reasoning.

### What Makes This Course Unique

- **Visual Learning**: Real clinical images and scenarios from actual newborn exams
- **Clinical Reasoning**: Practice the thought process behind diagnosis and management
- **AI-Powered Feedback**: Receive detailed evaluation of your assessment and decision-making
- **Comprehensive Coverage**: From APGAR scoring to neurological assessment
- **Evidence-Based**: All content aligned with current pediatric guidelines
- **Safe Learning Environment**: Practice identifying findings without risk to patients

## How the Course Works

### 1. Learn the Clinical Context
Each module introduces key concepts about newborn exam findings, normal variations, and red flags.

### 2. View Clinical Images & Scenarios
You'll see realistic clinical images and detailed patient scenarios (all de-identified and from public domain sources or educational collections).

### 3. Provide Your Assessment
For each case, you'll:
- Identify the finding(s)
- Determine if it's normal, benign, or concerning
- Explain your clinical reasoning
- Recommend appropriate next steps

### 4. AI-Powered Evaluation
Our LLM evaluation system analyzes your response and provides:
- Assessment of your identification accuracy
- Feedback on your clinical reasoning
- Evaluation of your management decisions
- Teaching points about the finding
- Guidance on parent communication
- Scores based on pediatric best practices

### 5. Learn from Detailed Feedback
Receive specific feedback on:
- What you identified correctly
- What you may have missed
- Why certain findings are significant
- When to escalate vs reassure
- Evidence-based management approaches

### 6. Practice Until You Master It
Revisit exercises, refine your assessment skills, and build confidence in newborn exam interpretation.

## Learning Objectives

By completing this course, you will be able to:

- ✅ Perform systematic newborn physical examinations
- ✅ Differentiate normal findings from pathological conditions
- ✅ Recognize variations that are benign vs those requiring intervention
- ✅ Make appropriate referral and management decisions
- ✅ Communicate findings effectively to families
- ✅ Apply evidence-based guidelines to newborn care
- ✅ Identify red flags requiring urgent evaluation

## Who Should Take This Course?

This course is designed for:

- Medical students preparing for pediatric rotations
- Pediatric residents building exam skills
- Family medicine residents seeing newborns
- Nurse practitioners and physician assistants in newborn care
- Anyone wanting to strengthen newborn assessment skills

## Technical Requirements

- Modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- WebGPU support for AI model (most modern browsers)
- ~2GB free RAM for optimal performance
- Internet connection for initial model download (then works offline)
- Device capable of displaying images clearly

## Image Attributions & Sources

All clinical images in this course are sourced from:
- Public domain medical image libraries (CDC, NIH, Wikimedia Commons)
- Creative Commons licensed educational resources (with proper attribution)
- De-identified educational photographs from open medical education projects

Full attribution and licensing information is available in our [Image Sources Documentation](/docs/newborn-exam-images/).

## Privacy & Ethics

- All patient images are fully de-identified or from public domain sources
- No protected health information (PHI) is included
- All cases are for educational purposes only
- Images are used in accordance with their respective licenses
- Content reviewed for cultural sensitivity and inclusivity
