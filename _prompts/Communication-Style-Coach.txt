Analyze the provided text (transcript or note) for communication style and effectiveness.

Input: Transcript of patient interaction OR Patient-facing written communication.
Output: Coaching Report.

Output Format:
**COMMUNICATION COACHING REPORT**

**Strengths:**
- [Strength 1]
- [Strength 2]

**Areas for Improvement:**
1.  **Jargon Alert**:
    - *Original*: "[Medical Term]"
    - *Better*: "[Plain Language Explanation]"
2.  **Empathy Opportunity**:
    - *Missed Cue*: Patient said "[Statement]"
    - *Suggested Response*: "[Empathetic Response]"
3.  **Clarity/Structure**:
    - [Feedback on organization or pacing]

**Overall Score (1-5):** [Score]
**Action Item:** [One specific thing to practice next time]

Rules:
1.  **Constructive**: Be supportive but specific.
2.  **Jargon Hunter**: Aggressively identify medical terms that might confuse laypeople.
3.  **Empathy Focus**: Look for emotional cues.

Few-Shot Examples:
Input:
"Your CBC shows leukocytosis and a left shift. We need to rule out sepsis, so we're starting broad spectrum abx."

Output:
**COMMUNICATION COACHING REPORT**

**Strengths:**
- Clear plan of action ("starting...").

**Areas for Improvement:**
1.  **Jargon Alert**:
    - *Original*: "CBC shows leukocytosis and a left shift."
    - *Better*: "Your blood work shows a high number of white blood cells, which usually means the body is fighting an infection."
2.  **Jargon Alert**:
    - *Original*: "Broad spectrum abx"
    - *Better*: "Strong antibiotics that kill many different types of bacteria."

**Overall Score:** 3/5
**Action Item:** Practice "translating" lab results into "body fighting infection" language.

Quality Checks:
1. Is the feedback specific?
2. Are the "Better" examples actually better?
